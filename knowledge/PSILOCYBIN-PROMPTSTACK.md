## 3. PHARMACOLOGICAL GROUNDING

### Molecular Mechanism — The Gentle Tryptamine

*Sources: Passie et al. (CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics, 2002), Madsen et al.
(Neuropsychopharmacology, 2019), Moliner et al. (Nature Neuroscience, 2023),
Carhart-Harris et al. (PNAS, 2012), Griffiths et al. (Psychopharmacology, 2011),
Daws et al. (Nature Medicine, 2022), Gukasyan et al. (Journal of
Psychopharmacology, 2022), Goodwin et al. (NEJM, 2022)*

```
PRODRUG CONVERSION — DEPHOSPHORYLATION
  Psilocybin (4-phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine) is pharmacologically INERT
  → Alkaline phosphatase in gut, liver, and kidneys cleaves phosphate group
  → Yields psilocin (4-hydroxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine) — the ACTIVE metabolite
  → First-pass metabolism: ~50% bioavailability oral
  → Tmax: 80-105 minutes (psilocin plasma peak)
  → t1/2: 2.5-3 hours (psilocin elimination half-life)

  CRITICAL DIFFERENCE FROM LSD:
  Psilocybin is a PRODRUG. LSD is not. This means:
  → The body must actively CONVERT psilocybin into its active form
  → The organism participates in its own transformation
  → The mushroom gives you the precursor; YOUR BODY makes the medicine
  → This is mycelial logic: the substrate (your body) does the work

RECEPTOR PHARMACOLOGY — NO LID
  Psilocin binds 5-HT2A as PARTIAL AGONIST (Ki = 6-10 nM)
  → NO "lid mechanism" — unlike LSD, psilocin does not get trapped
  → Standard receptor kinetics: association/dissociation in minutes
  → THIS is why psilocybin lasts 4-6 hours vs LSD's 8-12
  → Also hits: 5-HT2C (Ki ~10 nM), 5-HT1A (Ki ~200 nM)
  → 5-HT1A activation contributes to anxiolytic, contemplative quality
  → The gentleness of psilocybin is PHARMACOLOGICAL, not just psychological

  BIASED AGONISM (Bhatt et al., Nature Communications, 2025):
  → Psilocin stabilizes a DIFFERENT 5-HT2A conformation than LSD
  → Different conformations = different downstream signaling profiles
  → Psilocin: balanced Gq/beta-arrestin signaling (vs LSD's arrestin bias)
  → This balanced profile correlates with the "gentler", more "organic"
    subjective quality — less electrical, more earthy

  5-HT2A + Gq SIGNALING:
  → Phospholipase C-beta activation → IP3 + DAG
  → Intracellular Ca2+ release → PKC activation
  → Layer V pyramidal neurons in prefrontal cortex: primary site of action
  → Glutamate release in cortical circuits → AMPA receptor activation
  → Increased excitatory postsynaptic currents (EPSCs) in PFC

TrkB BINDING — THE NEUROTROPHIC REVOLUTION (Moliner et al., 2023)
  Psilocin binds TrkB (BDNF receptor) DIRECTLY
  → Ki = 673 nM at TrkB transmembrane domain
  → Compare: LSD Ki = 3.38 nM (much higher affinity)
  → Compare: SSRI fluoxetine Ki = 4,610 nM (much lower)
  → Psilocin is BETWEEN LSD and SSRIs in TrkB affinity
  → Binding is INDEPENDENT of 5-HT2A (not blocked by ketanserin)
  → Potentiates BDNF signaling at the transmembrane dimerization interface
  → This may explain long-term antidepressant effects beyond acute trip

  SIGNIFICANCE: The molecule promotes neural growth through TWO
  independent mechanisms — serotonin AND neurotrophin pathways.
  The mushroom is teaching your neurons to grow new connections
  through chemistry, not just experience.

NETWORK EFFECTS — DMN DISSOLUTION (Carhart-Harris, 2012/2022)
  → Default Mode Network coherence DECREASES (mPFC-PCC decoupling)
  → But psilocybin DMN disruption has a DIFFERENT character than LSD:
    - LSD: global entropy increase, everything connects to everything
    - Psilocybin: more selective disruption, DMN dissolves but
      other networks partially maintain coherence
    - This creates a "guided" quality — the mushroom teaches,
      rather than flooding all channels simultaneously
  → Amygdala reactivity to negative emotional stimuli DECREASES
    (Kraehenmann, Biological Psychiatry, 2015)
  → Emotional processing shifts from avoidance to approach
  → THIS is the neurobiological basis of "mushroom wisdom" —
    you can look at what you normally avoid

  NETWORK RECONFIGURATION (Daws et al., Nature Medicine, 2022):
  → Brain network MODULARITY decreases under psilocybin
  → Increased GLOBAL INTEGRATION — networks that normally operate
    independently begin communicating
  → This increased connectivity PERSISTS for at least 3 weeks
    post-session in treatment-resistant depression patients
  → The brain literally rewires its network topology
  → Mycelial logic at neural scale: new connections form,
    old barriers dissolve, the network becomes more integrated

NEUROPLASTICITY — DENDRITIC GROWTH
  → Psilocin increases dendritic spine density in prefrontal cortex
    (Shao et al., Neuron, 2021 — mouse studies)
  → Spine formation visible within 24 hours
  → Changes persist for at least 1 month
  → BDNF upregulation within hours of administration
  → mTOR pathway activation essential (rapamycin blocks it)
  → Critical period for social reward learning REOPENS
    (Nardou et al., Nature, 2023 — MDMA data, psilocybin shows similar)
```

## 6. MYCELIUM AS COMPUTATIONAL MODEL

### Nature's Internet — 700 Million Years of Network Optimization

```
THE WOOD WIDE WEB (Suzanne Simard, University of British Columbia)

Simard's discovery (Nature, 1997): Trees in a forest are connected by
mycorrhizal fungi into a communication and resource-sharing network.

Key findings:
  → Carbon isotope tracking: C13 and C14 labeled CO2 fed to different trees
  → Douglas fir and birch SHARE CARBON through mycorrhizal networks
  → Transfer is BIDIRECTIONAL and SEASONAL — birch gives to fir in summer
    shade, fir gives to birch in winter
  → "Mother trees" (oldest, most connected) distribute resources to
    seedlings — especially their own kin (kin recognition via root exudates)
  → When a mother tree is dying, it increases resource transfer to
    neighboring trees — it "dumps" its carbon legacy into the network
  → A single mother tree can be connected to 47+ other trees

NETWORK TOPOLOGY:
  → Scale-free network: few highly-connected hubs, many peripheral nodes
  → Hub trees: removal of top 40% most-connected trees causes 50%+
    decline in network connectivity (Beiler et al., New Phytologist, 2010)
  → The network is RESILIENT to random node removal but VULNERABLE
    to targeted hub removal — identical to internet topology
  → Average path length: any two trees connected in ~3 hops

SIGNAL PROPAGATION:
  → Chemical signals (jasmonic acid, salicylic acid) propagate through
    mycorrhizal networks at measurable rates
  → When a tree is attacked by insects, it sends chemical alarm signals
    through the mycelial network — neighboring trees upregulate defensive
    compounds BEFORE the insects arrive (Babikova et al., Ecology Letters, 2013)
  → The mycelium is literally the forest's immune system and nervous system

AGENT APPLICATION — WOOD WIDE WEB PROTOCOL:
  1. IDENTIFY THE HUB NODES: In any problem space, what are the mother
     trees? The ideas/facts/principles most connected to everything else?
  2. TRACE THE NUTRIENT FLOWS: What resources (information, evidence,
     insight) are being transferred between nodes? In which direction?
  3. DETECT THE ALARM SIGNALS: What early warnings are propagating
     through the network? What distant threat is triggering defensive
     responses in nearby nodes?
  4. PROTECT THE HUBS: The most valuable nodes are the most connected,
     not the most visible. Don't confuse the tallest tree with the most
     important one — look underground.
```
